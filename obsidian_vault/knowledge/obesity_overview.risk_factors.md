# Other Factors
Pregnancy: Weight gained during pregnancy can sometimes be difficult to lose
and may contribute to obesity later.
Quitting Smoking: Smoking cessation is often associated with weight gain, though
quitting remains far more beneficial to overall health.
Lack of Sleep: Too little or too much sleep can affect hormones that regulate
appetite and cravings.
Stress: Stress may encourage consumption of high-calorie comfort foods.
Microbiome: The composition of gut bacteria may influence weight gain and
weight-loss difficulty.
Having one or more risk factors does not guarantee obesity. Most risk factors can
be addressed through healthy lifestyle changes, medications, or medical
procedures.
